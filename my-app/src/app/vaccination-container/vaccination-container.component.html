Vaccination Effectiveness on ICU-Admissions

In Asia, high vaccination rates from 2021 to 2023 led to a decline in ICU Admissions, indicating vaccination effectiveness. However, sporadic spikes in admissions were linked to emerging COVID-19 variants. Analyzing policy responses, the reproduction rate, and economic factors can provide insights into ICU demand.
In Africa, a sustained increase in vaccination rates corresponded with a notable decrease in ICU Admissions, emphasizing the beneficial role of vaccination campaigns in reducing the need for intensive care.
In Europe from 2021 to 2023, while vaccination campaigns remained constant, substantial spikes in ICU Admissions could be attributed to the emergence of new and highly contagious COVID-19 variants, as well as the potential impact of changes in social distancing policies and other containment measures.
In North America, a similar pattern was observed with consistent vaccination campaigns from 2021 to 2023, leading to significant spikes in ICU Admissions, likely due to the emergence of new COVID-19 variants and changing social distancing policies. However, from 2023 onwards, there was a notable decrease in ICU Admissions, indicating the potential impact of increased vaccination coverage and evolving public health measures.
In South America, a substantial reduction in ICU Admissions during 2021, and the sustained low levels thereafter, can be attributed to the effective vaccination efforts in the region.
