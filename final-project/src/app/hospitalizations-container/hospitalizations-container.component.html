Vaccination Effectiveness on Hospitalizations

In Asia, consistent high vaccination rates from 2021 to 2023 have been associated with a general decline in hospitalizations, indicating the potential effectiveness of vaccinations in reducing the need for hospital care. However, occasional increases in hospitalizations may be linked to new COVID-19 variants, as well as other contributing factors such as government policy responses and healthcare infrastructure
In Africa, the continuous rise in vaccination rates corresponds with a notable decrease in hospitalizations, underscoring the positive impact of vaccination campaigns in reducing the need for hospital care.
Throughout 2021-2023 in Europe, despite consistent vaccination campaigns, substantial spikes in hospitalizations were observed. These spikes can be attributed to the emergence of new and highly contagious COVID-19 variants, as well as potential effects of social distancing policies and other containment measures. However, from 2023 onwards, there was a significant decrease in hospitalizations, potentially due to increased vaccination coverage and evolving public health measures.
Similar to Europe, North America experienced steady vaccination campaigns from 2021 to 2023, resulting in significant spikes in hospitalizations, possibly due to the emergence of new COVID-19 variants and changes in social distancing policies. However, from 2023 onwards, there was a notable decrease in hospitalizations, suggesting the potential impact of increased vaccination coverage and evolving public health measures.
In South America, a substantial reduction in hospitalizations during 2021 and sustained low levels afterward can be attributed to the effective vaccination efforts in the region, indicating that vaccinations have played a critical role in reducing the need for hospital care.
